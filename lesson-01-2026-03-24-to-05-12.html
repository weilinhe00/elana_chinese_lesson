🔤 拼音 pīnyīn · how Chinese sounds work
拼音 pīnyīn — the sound system of Chinese
声母 shēngmǔ · initial consonants (the beginning of a syllable)
lips 唇bpmf
tip 舌尖dtnl
back 舌根gkh
palate 舌面jqx
curl 翘舌zhchshr
flat 舌齿zcs
韵母 yùnmǔ · finals (the vowel ending of a syllable)
Single: a  o  e  i  u  ü
Compound: ai  ei  ao  ou  ia  ie  ua  uo
Nasal -n: an  en  in  un
Nasal -ng: ang  eng  ing  ong
special whole-syllable sounds — read each as one unit
zhi  chi  shi  ri zi  ci  si ye  wu  yu  yue
声调 shēngdiào · tones — every syllable has one!

tone 1 — flat
māo 猫

máo 毛

tone 3 — dip
mǎ 马

where to write the tone mark — 4 rules
1there is an a → mark the a: māo  hǎo  lái
2no a → mark o or e: wǒ  hē  zuò
3iu or ui → mark the last vowel: liú  guì
4single vowel → mark it directly: bǐ  lǜ
⚠️ tone change rule (变调 biàndiào)
when tone 3 + tone 3 come together, the first changes to tone 2:
nǐ hǎo → ní hǎo · 你好 hello
wǒ yě → wó yě · 我也 me too
